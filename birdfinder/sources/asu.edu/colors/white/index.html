Caspian Tern

Sterna caspia

Always associated with water, this large tern can be seen on river beaches, over lakes and marshes, or along the ocean throughout the world. It feeds on fish and crustaceans captured by diving headfirst into the water. Breeding colonies are on beaches and grassy hummocks near the water, and the nest is on the sand with some moss or grass. Great Egret

Ardea alba

Large and obvious, the yellow bill and dark legs of this white water bird make identification easy. The Great Egret occurs around the world in marshy areas, irrigation ditches, ponds and in tidal estuaries where it stalks fish, insects, crustaceans, frogs and small birds. It makes a large nest of twigs and branches and usually nests in colonies with other water birds, such as herons, pelicans and cormorants.
