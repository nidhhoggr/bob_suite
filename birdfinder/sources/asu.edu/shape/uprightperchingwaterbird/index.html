American Avocet

Recurvirostra americana

A noisy and obvious resident of inland marshes, mud flats and ocean estuaries, the American Avocet uses its long legs and peculiarly upturned bill to stir up aquatic invertebrates underwater. American Dipper

Cinclus mexicanus

Found exclusively on rushing mountain streams, this amazing bird is usually seen bobbing up and down on rocks in streams or using its buzzy and fast flight to fly low over the water. Double-crested Cormorant

Phalacrocorax auritus

A common species on inland lakes, rivers and ponds, it is also found in coastal habitats.
